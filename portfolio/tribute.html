APJ Abdul Kalam

11th President of India, Scientist, and Visionary

"Don't take rest after your first victory because if you fail in second, more people are there to say 'I told you so'."
Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He was a renowned scientist, engineer, and administrator who played a crucial role in the development of India's space program. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam was known for his simplicity, humility, and vision for a developed India.

Achievements:

  • Developed India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3
  • Played a key role in the development of India's nuclear program
  • Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds"
